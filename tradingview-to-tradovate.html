First, the fee everyone gets wrong.

Search "Tradovate API" and you'll hit scary numbers: a $290–500/month CME license on top of everything. Here's what that actually is: the CME fee applies only if you stream real-time market data through Tradovate's API. Alert-driven execution doesn't do that — your charts and data stay on TradingView, where you already pay for them, and the execution software only routes orders. What you actually pay Tradovate is its $25/month API add-on. That's it.

Cloud service or your own machine?

Most tools in this space are cloud relays: your alert goes to their servers, which hold your broker access and place orders from a datacenter IP. That's fine for a personal account. If you trade a funded account, it's a standing risk — several prop firms prohibit cloud-based copiers and VPS execution in their agreements, and the IP your orders come from isn't yours to control.

BridgePit takes the other route: a desktop app on the computer you already trade from. Orders leave from your own IP. Credentials stay encrypted on your machine. You stay the supervisor — kill-switch, per-strategy position caps, a daily loss stop, and an end-of-day flatten are always on. Rules differ by firm and change; check your firm's current policy either way.

Questions traders actually ask.

"My firm warned me about cloud copiers — can I still automate?"

The warnings target cloud services and VPS setups — execution you don't control, from IPs that aren't yours. Software on your own computer, supervised by you, from your own residential IP, is a different architecture. Check your firm's current rules; that stays your job.

"Do I need the $290 CME license?"

Not for this. It applies to streaming market data through the broker API. Your data stays on TradingView; BridgePit only routes orders. You pay Tradovate's $25/month API add-on.

"Do I need to code?"

No. Webhook URL + ready-made message template, both copy-paste. If your strategy already fires alerts, you're twenty minutes away.

"What happens when my internet drops?"

BridgePit reconnects continuously and alerts you by email and Telegram; the end-of-day flatten retries until your account is confirmed flat.
