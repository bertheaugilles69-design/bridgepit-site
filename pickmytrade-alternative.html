A PickMyTrade alternative that isn't another cloud.

PickMyTrade forwards your TradingView alerts to your broker through its servers. BridgePit does the same job with no servers in the middle: a desktop app on your own machine executes the alert, the order leaves from your own IP, and your broker login never leaves your computer. Same alerts — different architecture.

When PickMyTrade is the better pick.

Honest answer, because it's also our sales filter: if you run many accounts on their flat $50, need a broker we don't support yet, or want nothing running at home — machine off, cloud does everything — PickMyTrade fits you better today. BridgePit exists for one trader specifically: the one whose income depends on a funded account and who doesn't want their execution living on someone else's IP.

Switching takes one URL change.

  1. Install BridgePit and follow the 20-minute setup — it ends with a paper fill on your screen.
  2. Edit each TradingView alert: replace the PickMyTrade webhook URL with yours ending in /v2/add-trade-data. The message body stays exactly as it is.
  3. Watch a day of paper fills, then cancel the old subscription when you trust what you see.

Questions traders ask.

Can I keep my existing alert format?

Yes — BridgePit reads the PickMyTrade message format natively. One URL edit per alert, nothing else.

Is BridgePit fully automated?

Semi-automated by design: it executes, you supervise. Kill-switch, caps, daily loss stop, and end-of-day flatten are always on.

Is local execution allowed at my prop firm?

BridgePit runs on your own computer and sends orders from your own IP — no cloud server between your signals and your broker. Rules differ by firm and change, so always check your firm's current policy.

What if my internet drops mid-position?

BridgePit reconnects continuously and alerts you by email and Telegram; the end-of-day flatten retries until your account is confirmed flat.
